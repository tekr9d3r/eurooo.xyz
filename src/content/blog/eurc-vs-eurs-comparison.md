# EURC vs EURS: Which EUR Stablecoin Should You Choose?

When it comes to Euro stablecoins, two names dominate the market: **EURC** by Circle and **EURS** by Stasis. Both aim to maintain a 1:1 peg with the Euro, but they differ in key areas like regulatory compliance, DeFi integration, and chain availability.

This guide compares EURC and EURS to help you choose the right EUR stablecoin for your needs.

## EURC: The Circle Standard

**EURC** is issued by Circle, the same company behind USDC—one of the world's largest stablecoins. Circle's reputation for regulatory compliance and transparency makes EURC a trusted choice.

### EURC Strengths

- **Regulatory leadership**: Circle is MiCA-compliant and works closely with regulators
- **Deep DeFi integration**: Available on Aave, Morpho, and other major protocols
- **Multi-chain**: Native on Ethereum, Base, Avalanche, and Solana
- **Monthly attestations**: Grant Thornton audits reserves monthly
- **Strong liquidity**: Benefits from Circle's USDC infrastructure

### EURC Considerations

- Newer to market (launched 2022)
- US-based issuer may concern some European users

> **Current EURC Yield**: Earn up to 5.33% APY on EURC via [Morpho Gauntlet vault](/app)

## EURS: The European Pioneer

**EURS** by Stasis was one of the first EUR stablecoins, launching in 2018. Based in Malta, Stasis offers a European-first approach to Euro stablecoins.

### EURS Strengths

- **European issuer**: Malta-based with EU e-money license
- **Longest track record**: Operating since 2018
- **Proven stability**: Maintained peg through multiple market cycles
- **Direct redemption**: EUR withdrawal to bank accounts

### EURS Considerations

- Smaller DeFi footprint than EURC
- Fewer chain options
- Less frequent audits (quarterly vs. monthly)

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is EURC safer than EURS?

Both have audited reserves and regulatory oversight. EURC benefits from Circle's scale and monthly audits, while EURS has a longer operational history.

### Can I swap between EURC and EURS?

Yes, you can swap between them on decentralized exchanges like Curve or Uniswap, though liquidity varies.
