Earning yield on your Euros no longer requires a traditional bank account. With EUR stablecoins like EURC, you can access decentralized finance (DeFi) protocols that offer yields often exceeding what European banks provide—all while maintaining exposure to the Euro.

- **Lending**: Supply your tokens to borrowers and earn interest

## Risk Management

### Smart Contract Risk
- Stick to audited protocols with long track records
- Diversify across multiple protocols
- Start with smaller amounts

### Depeg Risk
- EUR stablecoins can temporarily lose their peg
- Larger, regulated issuers (Circle) have stronger peg mechanisms

### Yield Sustainability
- Very high APYs may not be sustainable
- Check TVL trends—growing TVL often means stable yield

### Protocol-Specific Risks
- Read documentation and understand each protocol's model
- Check audit reports and security practices

## Optimizing Your Yield

### Multi-Chain Strategy

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What's the minimum amount needed?

There's no protocol minimum, but consider gas costs. On Ethereum, deposits under €500-1000 may not be economical. Base and Gnosis have much lower gas costs.

### How often is yield paid?

Yield accrues continuously. You can withdraw your deposit plus earned interest at any time.

### Can I lose my principal?

In normal conditions, lending protocols preserve principal. However, extreme market events or smart contract bugs could result in losses.

### Is DeFi yield taxable?

In most European jurisdictions, yes. Consult a local tax advisor for your specific situation.
